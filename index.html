My Projects

Filipino Deepfake Audio Detection Tool Using Machine Learning, Deep Learning, and Transformer Models

2025
With deepfake audio becoming more prevalent and its potential misuse in fraud, misinformation, and identity theft, we developed the first-ever Filipino deepfake audio detection tool for our thesis project. Our approach combined deep learning models (ResNet-50, VGG-16), transformer models (Wav2Vec2, HuBERT), and machine learning models (Decision Tree, Extra Trees, Random Forest, Gradient Boosting). We trained these models on our own Filipino audio dataset to classify deepfake and genuine audio in the Filipino language. The best-performing models (ResNet-50, VGG-16, HuBERT, and Wav2Vec2) all achieved an impressive 100% accuracy in classification. Through this project, I gained in-depth knowledge of audio processing, deep learning, machine learning models, and transformer-based architectures. Additionally, I learned how to integrate machine learning models into a web application using Flask to address existing challenges in deepfake audio detection. .

Note: While the paper and project are complete, the thesis has not yet been presented. Therefore, the paper is not yet available. The final version will be posted here once finalized. If you have any questions or need more information, feel free to contact me directly!

Mobile Application for Household Waste Classification Using ResNet-50

2024
As part of a deep learning group project, we developed a mobile app for household waste classification. We trained a ResNet-50 model on our own dataset of 15,000 images with 15 household waste categories. The model achieved an impressive 93.88% training accuracy and 90.33% validation accuracy. Through this project, I deepened my understanding of training and fine-tuning deep learning models, dataset preparation, and model evaluation, as well as deploying them in mobile applications to address real-world problems like efficient waste management.
